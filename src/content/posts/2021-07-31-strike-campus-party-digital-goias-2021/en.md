I'm still processing what happened over the last few days: we took 1st place at Hackathon Reboot The World, during Campus Party Digital Goiás 2021.

This was yet another hackathon experienced in the pandemic context — remote team, lots of calls, lots of conversation, and that constant feeling that time is passing faster than it should. Even so, the challenge was one of those that remind you why you love technology: using software to answer a real problem.

The challenge was organized by Shawee and designed with input from Goiás Turismo, the state tourism agency. The proposal was to create a technological solution for nature tourism in Goiás, considering both the needs of tourists and the reality of business owners and managers of tourism establishments.

## What Strike was

Our project was called Strike. The core idea was a digital passport for tourists, combined with a management system for tourism business operators.

On one side, tourists could register their interests, travel needs, and preferences. On the other, tourism businesses could register their services and specifics. The solution aimed to connect supply and demand, helping people who want to explore Goiás find more suitable experiences and helping local entrepreneurs better promote what they offer.

At its heart, it was an attempt to answer a simple question: how do you make the tourism experience more connected, personalized, and useful for both sides?

## The team

I worked with Liggia Lima, Karla Pereira, and Josinaldo Júnior. It was a highly complementary team: business, marketing, development, product, and design all had to stay in constant conversation for the proposal to make sense.

In the project, I was responsible for design. That meant turning loose ideas into flow, interface, and visual narrative for the presentation. In hackathons, design isn't just about making things look good — it's about helping the team better explain the problem, the solution, and the value of the proposal.

The articles published by the Government of Goiás highlighted that Strike was chosen as the most complete solution for the presented needs. They also announced that the team won a five-week acceleration program at the Sai do Papel accelerator, support from Goiás Turismo for project development, and additional awards.

## What remains

Winning was incredible, but the most important thing was noticing the growth since previous hackathons. In 2019 came my first contact with this kind of marathon. In 2020 came the remote challenge, open data, and a team formed with people I was still getting to know. Now, in 2021, came first place.

This sequence reinforces something I've been learning in practice: good technology is born when the team can understand context, simplify decisions, and collaborate even under pressure.

Strike was a milestone for me because it brought together several things I consider important: applied technology, teamwork, design as a tool for clarity, and the desire to build something that can leave the presentation and find the real world.
